Julia G. Ko

Growing up in the Midwest and traveling extensively has taught me grit to tackle increasingly harder challenges. I've been fortunate to have had successful entrepreneurs and intellectuals as role models. My father provided the early foundation by encouraging my passions and providing opportunities to take continual risks.

I’ve built two startups from the ground up with one successful exit. I started my first company, an Asian food manufacturing business. We reached sales of over $1M in the first 1.5 years of business and sold the business after 3 years. I attended UCLA on a Recognition Scholarship as a transition.

In 2011, I co-founded SurePod in anticipation of the connected devices trend. The industry is now expected to reach $3 trillion by 2020. I enjoy building companies and useful products for consumers and the IoT market.

Gaijin Kitchen, Inc.

I founded and grew a food manufacturing company with multiple offices and retail operations in the Midwest. Our product success was, in part, due to an alliance rev-­share program with name brands such as WholeFoods Market, Dierberg’s and Compass Group. SurePod Corporation

SurePod Corporation
Connected World 2012 Award Winner


SurePod, a mini cellphone for the downmarket, functioned as a delivery mechanism for concierge and emergency services over the cellular network. The 1st gen cellphone was a Connected Conference 2012 winner and investor funded including Semyon Dukach, MD of Techstars in Boston. Star Trek, my mother and grandmother were sources of inspiration in creating the product.

I created, negotiated and managed the supply chain locally and bootstrapped the smart device for 100k, working with an engineering team and several contract manufacturers in Shenzhen. Working in parallel my co­founder, Steve and I pre sold the national dealer network, built the back­end infrastructure and managed our big four carrier relationships.

Most recently, Yves Behar’s designer and I developed the 2nd gen prototype with an LCD screen, GenesisPhone. With the help of our medical alarm dealers, we attacked our 1st vertical and delivering product to a $300+ million market and broke even with recurring subscription rev.

Vocale

Vocale


Using Near-Field Communication, we are taking dining, cafes and retail experiences digital. We are Yik Yak for businesses and consumers, with an effortless payment system.

Vocale enables restaurants, cafes and retail stores to have real time communication with their customers. With the tap of their phones, patrons can have a better, seamless dining, retail experience and operators have a community hub for their business. Moreover, operators can increase margins and customer retention. Pilot programs currently in the SF Bay Area.
