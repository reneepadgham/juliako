Growing up in the Midwest and traveling extensively has taught me grit to tackle increasingly harder challenges. My exchanges to Finland and China, backpacking Latin America sans local language skills or network have significantly shaped me. I've been fortunate to have grown up with successful entrepreneurs and intellectuals as role models. My father provided the early foundation, by encouraging my passions and giving me opportunities to take continual risks.

I’ve built two startups from the ground up with one successful exit. I started my first company, an Asian food manufacturing business. Our product success was, in part, due to an alliance partnership rev-share program with name brands such as WholeFoods Market, Dierberg’s and Compass Group. We reached sales of over $1M in the first 1.5 years of business. I sold the business after 3 years and attended UCLA on a Recognition Scholarship as a transition.

The IoT industry is expected to be $7.3 trillion by 2020, larger than the social media wave. Shortly after graduation, I co-founded SurePod. SurePod is a mini cellphone for the down-market (seniors & children) as a delivery mechanism for health and concierge services. I'm excited to leverage software and hardware to improve people's lives.

In my spare time, I enjoy reading novels, The Economist, MIT Technology Review, lifting free weights, adventure travel, intense hiking, gluten-free and spending time at the gym.

Projects

Gaijin Kitchen, Inc.
When I was 19, I visited San Francisco and noticed an Asian foods trend. I decided to capitalize on the fact trends move inward from coastal cities and dropped out of college after three weeks of classes.

I founded and grew a food manufacturing company with multiple offices and retail operations in the Midwest. My first hire was an Operations Manager from a national Los Angeles competitor who brought the operations manual with him. We reached sales of over $1M in the first 1.5 years of business through rev-share alliances with WholeFoods Market, Dierberg’s and Compass Group. Wanting a more dynamic industry, had a successful exit at age 22.

• Grew the team from 1 to 24 employees.
• Key Alliance Partners: WholeFoods market, Dierberg's Markets, Compass Group
• Created marketing strategy
• Managed key vendor relationships
• Grew business unit by 50% in year after acquisition.
• Provided tuition reimbursement for employees to further their education on a quarterly basis.
SurePod Corporation
SurePod, a mini cellphone for the downmarket, functioned as a delivery mechanism for concierge and emergency services over the cellular network. The 1st gen cellphone was a Connected Conference 2012 winner and investor funded including Semyon Dukach, MD of Techstars in Boston. Star Trek, my mother and grandmother were sources of inspiration in creating the product.

I created, negotiated and managed the supply chain locally and bootstrapped the smart device for 100k working with an engineering team and several contract manufacturers in Shenzhen. In retrospect, being tutored in Mandarin and calligraphy after school enabled me to build a device for a third of the cost.Working in parallel my co-founder, Steve and I pre sold the national dealer network, built the back-end infrastructure and managed our big four carrier relationships.The device was sold through medical alarm dealers and broke even with recurring subscription revenue.

Most recently, one of the leading industrial designers at Fuse Project and I developed the 2nd gen prototype with an LCD screen, GenesisPhone.With the help of our Dealers, we attacked our 1st vertical and delivering product to a $300+ million market and broke even with recurring subscription rev.

The platform and sensors enable management and operators to decrease overhead, increase margins and provide a better customer experience. Pilot programs currently in the SF Bay Area.
